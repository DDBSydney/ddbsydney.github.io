Westpac has released a new film for its Bump Account, via DDB Sydney, that takes a humorous look at parenting and highlights the importance of saving from an early age.

The ad is all about embracing all those parenting stuff ups along the way – because we all know there is no such thing as the perfect parent.

Parents all want to do the right thing by their kids, and help in every way they can, but inevitably nothing goes to plan.

Vomit, sleep deprivation, more vomit, vomit-inducing nappies. Being a parent requires unconditional love. Oh, and teaching your child the benefits of saving from an early age with Westpac’s Bump account.

In a country where housing affordability, wage growth and wealth are showing signs of going backwards, in their 200th year Australia’s oldest bank wanted to make a positive change.

We created a completely unique financial product to help a new generation get started – a savings account exclusively for young children. The Bump Account.

Parents across the nation signed their kids up. Or their friends and family told them to. Harnessing all the love and likes and turning them into a more secure financial future.

Like any new arrival, our account made a lot of noise.

+41 million media impressions. Generating 5.1 million in earned media.

Over 25, 000 new accounts registered. A 1200% increase in new accounts for kids. With 57% from non-Westpac customers.
