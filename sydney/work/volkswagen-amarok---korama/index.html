Volkswagen Amarok - ‘Korama’

BRIEF

The Volkswagen Amarok is a tough, award-winning ute, but as a newcomer with posh European styling, the Amarok was a long way from winning the trust of Australian tradesmen who rely heavily on their vehicles. But the introduction of a new, extremely powerful V6 variant made the Amarok the most powerful ute in its class. This gave us the opportunity to challenge perceptions. Our objective was two-fold; 1. As the only V6 ute we need to announce our news and claim category leadership. Pick-up considerers needed to know that the Volkswagen Amarok is changing the ute market in Australia. 2 - We also needed to get people excited, the power of a V6 is nothing short of extraordinary and we needed the communications to deliver on that and make them feel the thrill of the V6.

Solution / strategy

We challenged preconceptions. If Aussie drivers were judging our pick-up by its cover, we needed to change the cover. We enlisted the help of Mad Max concept designer, Peter Pound, and built a completely new shell for the Amarok V6. It oozed performance and now looked just as mean as the V6 engine that powered it.

Media

We then leaked spy shots to popular automotive sites. Editorial media channels spread the word and soon news of a mystery prototype being tested in the Australian bush appeared on blogs, forums and spy sites across the globe. Debate ignited as car fans guessed which company was behind the tough new vehicle. Then, in partnership with Australia’s most popular Spy Shot website, we were able to retarget everyone who engaged with our campaign, revealing that the powerful mystery prototype was in fact an Amarok V6 in disguise.

Activation

To prove our capabilities we invited four unsuspecting performance enthusiasts to put the prototype to the test. With no preconceptions holding them back, the drivers were amazed by its power but were even more surprised when we revealed that this mean off-roader was actually the Amarok V6 in disguise.

OUTCOME/RESULTS

Provide evidence of how successfully your objectives were achieved; what was the response rate and how was it measured? (3000 characters max). Not only did we announce the launch of the new Amarok V6, generating a significant amount of intrigue and excitement but we also sold some utes. The Amarok V6 model made up 38% of the Amarok sales in the first month and has broken Amarok sales records every month since launch. In Australia, the word “ute” refers to a pick-up truck, mostly used by tradesmen. The Amarok is the newest model in this category and out audience is skeptical about its credentials.

LOCATION/PLATFORM (PR):

Our strategy was to spread the news of our ‘mystery prototype’ through platforms where enthusiasts were already debating the origin of secret camouflaged prototypes. As we leaked spy footage and images, the enthusiasts leapt at the chance to give their opinion as to what this ‘thing’ was. On these platforms, they engaged and shared our campaign organically. From Manchester to Moscow, the world was talking about a prototype that wasn’t actually a prototype. Then, by retargeting our audience on various other channels, including YouTube and Facebook, we revealed that the powerful mystery prototype was actually an Amarok V6 in disguise.
